A little more about me

My interest in IT started at a young age, it would have been around 10/11 years old. I started playing The Sim, begging my parents for every expansion, and then spending hours on the computer just playing. This progressed deeper into gaming as I got older and resulted in enough technical ability to build and troubleshoot my own gaming devices.

After I had my first child, I was trying to get back into the work force and a job in IT came up at a local university and my passion has only grown from there. I love the rush of fixing or creating something, working out something no one else can figure out how to do.

From my start in IT Support on a Service Desk I moved through several
different AV and IT roles. Ultimately, I wound up support the online learning management system as well as the lecture capture system for a major university in NSW. From there I moved into a projects team where I worked with a team to implement the lecture capture system from the company I now work for.

I chose RMIT for 2 reasons. It is a technology university and I want a technology degree. What better place to get a technology degree than a well-respected technology university? The rest of my choice was driven purely by requirement. Due my career and family commitments I absolutely require the ability to study online with a workload that suits me.

I would like to solidify and build on my current IT knowledge, fill the gap where others have education and I only have work experience and experience the ‘uni’ experience I didn’t have when I was younger.
